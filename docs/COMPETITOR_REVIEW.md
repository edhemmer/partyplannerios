# Competitor Review

This review summarizes the feature lanes occupied by current event and party planning apps and where Party Planner should be stronger.

## Competitor Strengths

- Partiful: fun invite pages, link-based RSVP, text blasts, reminders, polls, emojis/GIFs, photo sharing, payment collection, and social energy.
- Apple Invites: polished iOS experience, Maps, Weather, Apple Music playlists, shared albums, AI-generated invitation visuals, and easy RSVP for Apple users.
- Evite and Paperless Post: invitation templates, RSVP tracking, reminders, and broad user familiarity.
- Howbout and shared calendar apps: group scheduling, shared calendars, polls, and social coordination.
- Wedding planning apps: checklists, budgets, guest lists, vendors, timelines, and milestone planning.
- Splitwise-style tools: clean shared expense tracking and balance clarity.

## Product Gap

Most competitors do not operate as a trusted live execution system. They help people announce, invite, RSVP, or track tasks, but they rarely answer:

- Is the plan current?
- Who changed what?
- Which assignments are unowned?
- Which expenses lack receipt evidence?
- Which edits are conflicted?
- Is the event ready right now?
- What should the host do next?

## Party Planner Advantage

Party Planner should compete by being the operational source of truth:

- Realtime sync status.
- Trust score.
- Audit trail for important changes.
- Receipt evidence.
- Assignment coverage.
- Conflict review.
- RSVP confidence tied to quantities and budget.
- Run-of-show execution timeline.
- Next best actions.
- Permission-aware editing by role.

## Design Direction

Keep the app fun and attractive, but make reliability visible. Hosts should feel calm because they can see the plan is live, accurate, and accountable.
