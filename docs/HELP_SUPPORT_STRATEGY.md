# Help And Support Strategy

The app should teach users while they work. Party planning can become stressful, so help content must be short, searchable, and tied to the current workflow.

## In-App Support Goals

- Explain app terms in plain language.
- Show role-specific guidance for owners, cohosts, helpers, and guests.
- Teach users how to create events, assign work, track receipts, and manage updates.
- Reduce host confusion around expenses, split rules, RSVPs, and permissions.
- Help new users understand why the app is different from invite-only or checklist-only tools.

## Support Content Types

- Quick training cards for first-time users.
- How-to guides with numbered steps.
- Definitions with examples.
- Best practices for communication, expenses, and event-day execution.
- Troubleshooting topics for missing RSVPs, messy plans, blocked responsibilities, and unclear costs.

## Initial In-App Topics

- Create a strong event plan.
- Invite helpers without losing control.
- Update your assigned work.
- Track receipts and split costs.
- Understand roles and permissions.
- Fix a messy or incomplete plan.
- Know what others can edit.

## Future Support Features

- Contextual help buttons on each screen.
- First-run guided tour for owners.
- Helper onboarding after accepting an invite.
- Searchable FAQ from remote content.
- Contact support ticket form.
- AI help assistant trained on the app workflow and event data permissions.
- Short event-day checklist mode.

## Tone

Support should feel calm, direct, and practical. Avoid jargon unless it is defined. Keep each guide focused on what the user is trying to accomplish right now.
