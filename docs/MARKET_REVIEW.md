# Market Review

This review summarizes the feature lanes occupied by current event and party planning apps without naming or borrowing from any competitor identity.

## Market Strengths

- Invite-first tools: custom event pages, link-based RSVP, guest reminders, polls, media sharing, and lightweight social interaction.
- Ecosystem invite tools: polished mobile invite creation, maps, weather, music, photo sharing, and calendar handoff.
- Traditional invitation tools: templates, RSVP tracking, reminders, and broad user familiarity.
- Shared calendar tools: group scheduling, availability, polls, and social coordination.
- Wedding and formal-event tools: checklists, budgets, guest lists, vendors, timelines, and milestone planning.
- Shared expense tools: group expense tracking, splits, balances, and reimbursement clarity.

## Product Gap

Most tools do not operate as a trusted live execution system. They help people announce, invite, RSVP, or track tasks, but they rarely answer:

- Is the plan current?
- Who changed what?
- Which assignments are unowned?
- Which expenses lack receipt evidence?
- Which edits are conflicted?
- Is the event ready right now?
- What should the host do next?

## Party Planner Advantage

Party Planner should compete by being the operational source of truth:

- Realtime sync status.
- Trust score.
- Audit trail for important changes.
- Receipt evidence.
- Assignment coverage.
- Conflict review.
- RSVP confidence tied to quantities and budget.
- Run-of-show execution timeline.
- Next best actions.
- Permission-aware editing by role.

## Design Direction

Keep the app fun and attractive, but make reliability visible. Hosts should feel calm because they can see the plan is live, accurate, and accountable.
